01. Why this matters

The Problem with Jumping Time

Nature does not execute sudden jumps. In the field of chronobiology, researchers have shown that the human body naturally synchronizes its internal circadian rhythm to the gradual, daily shift of the sunrise.

Twice a year, however, we force a jarring 60-minute time shift on ourselves. This abrupt transition severs the alignment between our biological "sun clock" and our "social clock," causing what scientists call Social Jetlag. This artificial shift is linked to acute sleep loss, increased cardiovascular events, and massive disruptions to digital infrastructure (like smart alarms triggering late or automated routines overlapping).

smoothtime.io solves this by mimicking nature's gradual "astro-shift" on your network. By spreading the transition across the entire season—adjusting just a fraction of a second each day—your devices stay perfectly aligned with Daylight Saving Time without ever experiencing the shock of a sudden jump.

02. How it Works

timeline

Gentle Adjustments

Instead of waiting months to move your clock by a full hour, our open-source time servers gradually shift the time throughout the year. It works just like a physical clock moving very slightly faster or slower, entirely unnoticed by users.

03. Setup for your Devices

Integration is easy. Simply tell your computer, router, or smart home hub to get its time from us instead of the default manufacturer servers.

For Home Users (Routers & Smart Hubs)

Log into your Wi-Fi router or smart home dashboard. Look for "Time Settings" or "NTP Server" and paste the address shown on the right.

For System Administrators

Update your standard network configuration files. We highly recommend keeping a standard time pool (like pool.ntp.org) configured as a secondary fallback.

Server Address 
# Your regional smoothtime server:
europe-central.smoothtime.io

# Example Linux (ntp.conf) integration:
server europe-central.smoothtime.io iburst
pool pool.ntp.org iburst
